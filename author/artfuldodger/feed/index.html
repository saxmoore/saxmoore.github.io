]]> Give your home the extra protection that it needs by installing reliable electric fence.

I’m sure we all feel as though we are living in a Wild West country these days. Gunfights on the streets and daily news about how dangerous it is becoming out there really puts a damper on the way in which we live our lives. Our thoughts immediately turn to the worst case scenarios that our family could find themselves in should we not have all of the latest and greatest safety mechanisms in place. Electric fencing in Johannesburg is really one of the few security measures that we absolutely need to have in place to have peace of mind.

As a South African homeowner living in Johannesburg, the chances are that you already have an electric fence in place. And this is fantastic because you have recognized the importance of having electric fencing in Johannesburg to keep you and your family safe, or to keep your business safe.

But did you know that once an electric fence has been installed, you will need to make sure that you keep an eye on it. I don’t need to tell you that the electric fence you install needs to be operating efficiently in order for it to serve its purpose of keeping you safe. Once you start feeling really secure, you might think that you can get away with turning it off or you might think that just because the fence is up but not on is enough to keep criminals at bay.

You are wrong to think this and this is why

Criminals are far more intelligent than you and I would like to think. Remember that they make a career out of stealing property and terrorizing people, often without even being seen and in South Africa we know that they do these illegal acts without ever being caught. So please don’t take comfort in thinking that the average criminal who is going to break into your home is anything less than a mastermind who is keeping a careful watch on your home as he plans his invasion.

“The drawback of stealing a thing, is that one never knows how wonderful the thing that one steals is.”
– Oscar Wilde

Criminals do not care about the sentimental value that you place on those items that they have stolen. They are only out to steal your valuables with the aim of selling them for as much as they can get. So protecting that which has a lot of valuable meaning to you is of the utmost importance.

Criminals pay far more attention to your home than you would like to think. This very thought is enough to make us incredibly paranoid, when we have electric fencing in Johannesburg our peace of mind is returned to us. But you can only be confident of your protection if you make sure that your electric fencing is active.

Now back to the average criminal being very clever in their current “profession”.

They keep a look out for the Johannesburg electric fencing that is no longer working or being used and they especially keep a look out for the fencing that has been turned off. They know that if a person has turned off their fencing that they are likely to be feeling safe and that they have let their guard down.

How do they know that the fencing has been turned off? Well, as a South African you have probably heard that electrical clicking sound that comes from an active electric fence. While you might think that this means that there is something wrong with the fencing, it actually means that the fencing is doing its job.

And criminals know that when that gentle but menacing clicking stops, your electric fencing is no longer working. And this is when they start plotting their invasion of your home. How can you keep the thieves away? Make a conscious effort to always listen for the clicking. If one day you no longer hear that click, get on the phone to the company that installed the fence and ask for help. A broken electric fence can happen, although it is something that happens very rarely.

The other reason that the clicking of the fence might have stopped is because the fence has been turned off. Maybe you have decided that you want to start saving electricity. After all, you haven’t had any trouble with criminals and they won’t know that you have turned off the fence, right? But a criminal is going to see that your fence is no longer on and that your defenses are down. So while you might be saving electricity by turning off your fence, you are actually putting yourself at risk.

The next thing that the not so sophisticated criminal is going to watch for is loose wires on your electric fence. Once they see that the wires have fallen down or become even slightly loose, they are immediately going to know that your fencing is broken and no longer effective.

This is when they take the opportunity to pounce.

Attacking your fence

Not all criminals are going to wait for your fence to break as they know that there is a great possibility of the fence never breaking and so they choose to physically attack the fencing. They take an object and they strike one or two insulators on the brackets with the aim of breaking them or they even go so far as to attack the wires. They then hide somewhere to see it the alarm goes off. You might be wondering just how you will be able to protect yourself when they do this. Taking the time to walk the perimeter of the fence once a week will help you in finding if there are places where the criminals are trying to get in. If you notice a break call the company which installed the fence as soon as possible to get it fixed.
